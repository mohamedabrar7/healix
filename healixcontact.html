Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our services and policies.

Transferring your prescription is easy. You can either bring your current prescription bottle to any of our locations, call us with your prescription information, or ask your doctor to send a new prescription directly to us. Our staff will handle the rest of the process for you.

Yes, we offer free medication delivery within a 5-mile radius of any of our pharmacy locations. For locations outside this range, a small delivery fee may apply. Deliveries are typically made within 24 hours of prescription processing.

We accept most major insurance plans including Medicare, Medicaid, Blue Cross Blue Shield, Aetna, Cigna, UnitedHealthcare, and many others. If you're unsure whether we accept your insurance, please contact us with your insurance details, and we'll be happy to verify coverage for you.

You can refill your prescription in several ways: through our mobile app, on our website, by calling our automated refill line, or by visiting any of our pharmacy locations in person. For regular medications, we also offer an auto-refill program that automatically processes your refills before you run out.

Yes, our pharmacists are certified to administer a variety of vaccines including flu, pneumonia, shingles, COVID-19, and travel vaccines. Most vaccinations are available on a walk-in basis, though we recommend calling ahead to ensure availability, especially for travel vaccines which may require special ordering.
